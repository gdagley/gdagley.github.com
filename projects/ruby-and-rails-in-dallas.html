The question is often asked "What does the Ruby and Rails scene look like in Dallas?" The answer simple: It is thriving! With a growing local user group in the Dallas Ruby Brigade, more and more people are finding that Ruby and Rails can speed web development and create happy programmers.

Local Ruby and Rails Jobs

Every week local jobs are popping up on some of the major job boards for Ruby and Rails developers, like the 37signals Gig Board and The Ruby / Rails Job Site. The Dallas Ruby Brigade has an active mailing list, irc channel, and a wiki.

Local Ruby and Rails Projects

Each month at DemoCampDallas, new projects are shown off, many which are built by local companies using Ruby and Rails to fuel their development. You can also check out the Dallas Ruby Brigade's wiki to see more local projects.

Local Ruby Celebrities

Dallas and Fort Worth has its own share of local Ruby and Rails celebrities. Here are just a few:
  • Dave Thomas - Pragmatic Publisher - publishes the Pickaxe book and Agile Web Development with Rails
  • Glenn Vanderburg - speaker on the No Fluff, Just Stuff tours
  • Adam Keys - creator of the infamous one-man show
